### The morning catch

The boats came in before dawn, their hulls crusted with salt and barnacles. Maria stood at the harbour wall counting them — six, where there should have been seven.

She pulled her coat tighter. The wind off the Atlantic had teeth this morning, and the fish market wouldn't open for another hour. Plenty of time to worry.

"Your father's boat is the slow one," said old Tomas from his bench. "Always has been. He stops to argue with the sea."

Maria didn't smile. Tomas said this every morning, and every morning it failed to help.
