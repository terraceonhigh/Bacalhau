### Salt and Patience

They split the bacalhau in the stone shed behind the market, Maria holding the knife while her father guided the blade along the spine. The flesh opened white and clean.

Twenty-eight days in salt. That was the rule. Her grandmother's rule, her great-grandmother's rule, all the way back to women whose names nobody remembered but whose method endured.

"The secret," her father said, packing coarse salt into the cavity, "is that there is no secret. Just salt and patience."

Maria wrote it down in her notebook anyway. Some things need to be written to become real.
