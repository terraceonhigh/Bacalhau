### The seventh boat

The hull appeared through the fog at half past five, listing slightly to starboard. Maria could see her father at the tiller, one hand raised — not waving, just acknowledging that the world still existed and he was part of it.

The catch was poor. Three crates of sardines and a single bacalhau, enormous and silver-grey, laid across the deck like a fallen king.

"One fish," her father said, climbing onto the dock. "But what a fish."

He smelled of brine and diesel and the particular exhaustion that comes from fighting something you love.
